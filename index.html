Prevention rules
  • Never increase weekly km more than 10% week-on-week
  • Every 4th week is a deload — cut volume 30–40%
  • Resting HR 8+ bpm above normal: take the day off
  • Pain above 3/10 in any joint: stop the session
  • Soreness in muscle = ok. Pain in tendon or joint = stop
  • Never run two hard sessions back-to-back
  • Warm up 10 min easy before every speed session
  • Cool down 10 min easy + 5 min stretching after every run
1–3 days of discomfort
  • Swap hard session for easy or rest
  • Ice 15 min after activity (not before)
  • Continue strength work if pain-free
  • Resume plan if pain gone within 3 days
4+ days or recurring pain
  • Stop running. Maintain fitness with cycling or swimming
  • See a physio before resuming
  • Re-enter plan 2 phases back
  • Losing 2 weeks ≠ losing the goal. Panic does.
Weekly schedule structure
  • Saturday — Long run
  • Sunday — Rest
  • Monday — Intervals (hard)
  • Tuesday — Rest
  • Wednesday — Easy run
  • Thursday — Threshold / tempo
  • Friday — Rest (or Strength session C)
Guide
VO2 max training
What you need to know before your first high-intensity session.

First session

Start with 4 × 3 min at 90–95% max HR. Equal rest between reps. Leave reps in the tank — the goal is completion, not heroics.

Progressive overload

Add one rep per week, or increase rep duration. Never increase both at once. Deload every 4th week — cut volume by 40%.

10 km race

A 10 km is run almost entirely at VO2 max intensity. Target sessions: 6 × 1 km at race pace with 90 sec recovery.

Half marathon

HM sits at lactate threshold. VO2 max sessions raise your ceiling so threshold pace feels easier. Add cruise intervals: 3 × 10 min at HM pace.
